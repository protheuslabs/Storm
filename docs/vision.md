# Vision

Storm exists to let people coordinate valuable work without surrendering most of the upside to a centralized corporate intermediary.

Traditional organizations often bundle coordination, management, trust, review, legal structure, and payment into one company. That company then captures a large share of the value created by workers. Storm's thesis is that much of this intermediary function can be turned into transparent software, crowd validation, and reputation systems.

Storm's deeper creative thesis is that latent ideas are an enormous untapped resource. The product should make it much easier for a thought, prompt, goal, or creative seed to become real work, real output, and real value.

The endgame is not a better freelance marketplace. The endgame is a work model where people can do all of their work through crowdsourced projects, and the corporate paradigm ceases to be the default structure for organizing human labor.

Storm's economic structure mixes market incentives with commons-oriented stewardship. Contributors should be rewarded for value creation, but ownership should not become a weapon that lets any party withhold value created by the network.

Storm's socioeconomic thesis is that contribution-based value routing can resolve problems that neither ownership-dominated capitalism nor centrally controlled communism has solved well.

## Mission

Help people create, coordinate, validate, and get paid through crowdsourced projects where contributors keep the overwhelming majority of the value they generate.

## Foundational Beliefs

- Creative ideas can have value.
- Useful positive action should be compensable.
- Coordination should not require surrendering most of the upside.
- Contributors should benefit when later work builds on what they created.
- People should be able to contribute flexibly, from small tasks to deeper project ownership.
- Creative input can compound when the system preserves attribution and routes downstream value back to contributors.
- AI training should not erase the people whose work created the underlying capability.
- Ownership is a legal interface, not the deepest primitive. Provenance and value routing matter more.
- Verified contribution is the central economic primitive.

## Long-Term Outcome

Storm should make it possible for a project to move from idea to execution through an open network of contributors:

- A user records an idea, goal, problem, or opportunity.
- A project owner defines an objective and budget.
- Storm decomposes the objective into small executable tasks.
- Contributors claim work that fits their skills and availability.
- Reviewers validate the work against clear acceptance criteria.
- The value ledger records what was produced, who contributed, who reviewed it, and how value was distributed.
- Contributors build portable reputation from verified work.

In later versions, Storm may also track residual value when later work builds on earlier ideas, research, decomposition, or execution.

Storm should also preserve provenance when AI systems train on Storm data or approved data from the broader Protheus Labs ecosystem. If AI-generated value can be traced back to source contributions, the originators should be eligible for compensation.

The long-term ideal is that fewer ideas die as passing thoughts. A user should be able to plant a creative seed and have Storm help turn it into executable work.

At full maturity, Storm should be capable of organizing enough types of work that a person can build a career, income stream, reputation, and creative legacy without needing a conventional employer.

In later infrastructure phases, Storm may transition onto the Protheus network and use InfRing primitives for receipts, deterministic policy, local validation, node identity, and federated trust.

At sufficient scale, Storm may need a release switch: a transition where Protheus Labs remains the origin point and may retain a transparent origin-value claim, but no longer has unilateral control over the network.

## What Storm Replaces

Storm is not a freelance marketplace with nicer economics. It is intended to absorb pieces of:

- Project management.
- Middle management.
- Recruiting and staffing.
- Work verification.
- Progress reporting.
- Contributor reputation.
- Payment allocation.
- Dispute handling.
- Career development.
- Organizational structure.

In the broader vision, Storm may also replace pieces of early company formation, venture coordination, public problem discovery, and real-world bounty routing. Those are later layers, not MVP requirements.

## Value Distribution Goal

Storm should be designed around the principle that the platform exists to coordinate work, not to own the value created by workers.

The aspirational target is for contributors and reviewers to keep roughly 95% of the value generated, with the platform taking only what is needed to operate, protect participants, and keep incentives healthy.

## Non-Negotiables

- Work must be broken into clear, executable units.
- Acceptance criteria must be visible before work begins.
- Payout logic must be visible before work begins.
- Review must be accountable.
- Reputation must be earned from verified outcomes.
- Platform fees must be transparent.
- AI training and data usage rights must preserve provenance.
- Legal wrappers, patents, and businesses must not be allowed to withhold value from contributors.
- Any future release switch must remove hidden control without removing accountability.
- The system must resist low-quality work, fake review rings, and value extraction by hidden intermediaries.

## Product North Star

The simplest expression of Storm is:

> Define valuable work, split it into executable tasks, let capable people complete it, validate the result, and route value transparently to the people who created it.

## Vision Layers

Storm should be documented in layers so the project can stay ambitious without becoming unbuildable:

- MVP: digital projects, tasks, review, ledger, and reputation.
- Platform: groups, idea containers, contribution graphs, and richer governance.
- Expansion: creative seeds, residual compensation, AI training provenance, public problem discovery, intelligence inputs, and advanced matching.
- Research: real-world bounties, robotics, fabrication, and institution-scale coordination.
- Endgame: a general work model that makes corporate employment optional or obsolete for most contributors.
- Substrate: future Protheus/InfRing network primitives for receipt-backed, user-owned coordination.
- Release: eventual transition from Protheus Labs-operated platform to network-governed system.
